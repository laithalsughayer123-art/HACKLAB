What Is HackLab

Discover the power of our comprehensive cybersecurity platform

First released in 2025, HackLab was developed as an all-in-one ethical hacking platform designed to bring together the most powerful open-source cybersecurity tools. It is completely free and available to the community, actively maintained by cybersecurity students and professionals alike.

HackLab aims to simplify penetration testing by providing a curated suite of tools across all phases of the ethical hacking lifecycle. From information gathering to exploitation and reporting, it's a one-stop solution for hands-on cybersecurity learning and research. The project is supported by a passionate team committed to security education.

Our platform integrates cutting-edge security tools with an intuitive interface, making complex security assessments accessible to both beginners and experienced professionals.

Penetration Testing Lifecycle

The five phases of ethical hacking with HackLab tools

Reconnaissance

Gathering information about the target using public or network-based techniques. It's the first step in ethical hacking where we collect data to identify potential vulnerabilities.

Scanning

Identifying live systems, open ports, and services on the target network using tools like Nmap and Nessus. This phase helps us understand the attack surface.

Gaining Access

Exploiting discovered vulnerabilities to gain control of the system using payloads and exploits. This is where we attempt to breach the system's defenses.

Maintaining Access

Creating backdoors or persistent mechanisms to retain access to the system for future use, simulating what real attackers would do to maintain their foothold.

Clearing Tracks

Hiding the intrusion by deleting logs, masking tools, and wiping digital footprints to demonstrate how attackers conceal their activities.
